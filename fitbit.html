MINDFUL STEPS DESIGNS

Background

Mindful Steps is a project that focuses on increasing the health of veterns through walking and Tai Chi. They use an algorithm to find the ideal number of steps these people should be taking a day, and use FitBits to monitor them. The current website for this project is not user friendly and has been getting low interaction from their audience.

Solution

A redesigned desktop and mobile friendly website was created

Research

To begin this discovery, I met with stakeholders and SMEs to gather information about the target audience for this project.
I identified our target audience to be:
  • Age: 50 years plus
  • Gender: More male than female
  • Primary Location: Boston VA
  • Co-morbid conditions: COPD, CHF, Diabetes

Interviews

I then launched 5 interviews with individuals who fit the target audience, and asked key questions pertaining to website usability, painpoints, and optimal structure.
Key insights I gained from these interviews include:
  • Many individuals do not prefer dropdowns, accordians, or other elements that require interaction.
  • The easiest website designs to learn have consistent structure with other websites they use (AARP, Google, Facebook)
  • Visual and text heiarchies are necessary to understand the organization of the websites

User Needs

Personas were created, and the user needs identified.
Top User Needs:
  • I need a website that does not require a lot of interaction, as this can be confusing and lead to missed information
  • I need a website that is visually pleasing so that I want to revisit it.
  • I need a website that has clear information, so that I can easily find what I am looking for without getting frustrated.

High Fidelity Prototype

Many of the web pages were redesigned and presented to the stakeholders. The full prototype cannot be shared due to privacy concerns.
Design Decisions:
  • The aim was to keep the redesign as similar to the original as possible, as resources for the redesign were limited.
  • A clearer page hierarchy was created, included a simplified header, creation of a footer, and decluttering of links.
  • The research team needed a weekly motivator for their participants, so a star system was created and emphasized at the top of the page.

Mobile View Creation

A new mobile view was created, as one did not exist before. Again, the full prototype cannot be shared due to privacy concerns.
Design Decisions:
  • The aim was to keep the mobile design as similar to the desktop design as possible, so that users did not have to relearn a new platform and felt comfortable with using the site on smaller screens.
  • The desktop's three column grid was changed to a two column grid, so that the content appears large enough on smaller devices to be usable.
  • The research team needed a weekly motivator for their participants, so a star system was created and emphasized at the top of the page.
