MINDFUL STEPS DESIGNS

Project Overview

The purpose of the the Mindful Steps research study is to examine ways to increase physical activity in veterns with Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) and chronic musculoskeletal pain. To do so, they utalize a combined website, pedometer, and Tai Chi program. This study is funded by VA Rehabilitation Research & Development. Today, the current website suffers from low user interaction and participants have reported usability issues.

Solution

A comprehensive redesign was implemented to create a user-friendly desktop and mobile website in the hopes of alleviating user frustration and inceasing participation.

Research Phase

Conducted stakeholder interviews and engaged subject matter experts (SMEs) to understand the target audience.

Key insights:
  • Preference against complex UI elements like dropdowns and accordions.
  • Desire for a familiar website structure akin to platforms like AARP and Google.
  • Importance of clear visual and text hierarchies for intuitive navigation.

User Needs Analysis

Developed personas and identified critical user needs.

Top user requirements:
  • Minimized interaction for ease of use.
  • Appealing visual design to encourage revisits.
  • Clear information presentation to avoid frustration during use.

High Fidelity Prototype

Redesigned multiple web pages and presented prototypes to stakeholders, emphasizing privacy concerns.

Design decisions:
  • Minimal deviation from original design to optimize resource utilization.
  • Established clear page hierarchy, streamlined header and footer, and reduced cluttered links.
  • Implemented a weekly motivator system using star ratings to engage participants.

Mobile Interface Design

Introduced a mobile-friendly interface, maintaining consistency with the desktop version to ensure user comfort on smaller screens.

Design adjustments:
  • Adapted desktop's three-column grid to a two-column layout for improved mobile usability.
